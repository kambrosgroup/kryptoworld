## Ledger: The Feature King

Ledger is the market leader for good reason. Their devices support more cryptocurrencies, have better mobile integration, and offer additional services through Ledger Live.

### Ledger Nano X ($149)

**Best for:** Mobile users, altcoin holders, those wanting an all-in-one solution

**Key advantages:**
- Bluetooth connectivity (use with phone)
- 8-hour battery life
- Supports 5,500+ cryptocurrencies
- Excellent Ledger Live mobile app
- Can install 100+ apps simultaneously

**The trade-offs:**
- Closed-source firmware (security through obscurity)
- 2020 data breach (customer info leaked, no funds affected)
- More complex interface

### Ledger Nano S Plus ($79)

**Best for:** Budget-conscious buyers, Bitcoin/Ethereum holders

The Nano S Plus is the sweet spot for most users. It has the same security as the X, supports the same coins, but lacks Bluetooth and battery. You connect via USB-C.

**My recommendation:** Start here unless you specifically need mobile connectivity.

## Trezor: The Open Source Champion

Trezor was the first hardware wallet (2014). Created by SatoshiLabs, it's fully open source and has never suffered a data breach.

### Trezor Model T ($179)

**Best for:** Security purists, Bitcoin maximalists, those wanting touchscreen

**Key advantages:**
- Fully open source (firmware + hardware)
- Color touchscreen (easier to verify addresses)
- No battery (less attack surface)
- Shamir backup support (advanced recovery)
- No customer data breaches ever

**The trade-offs:**
- More expensive
- Fewer supported coins
- No Bluetooth
- Weaker mobile support

### Trezor Safe 3 ($79)

## Security Comparison

Both devices are extremely secure when used correctly. Neither has ever been remotely hacked.

### Ledger Security Model
- Secure Element chip (bank-grade)
- Closed-source firmware
- Physical tamper resistance
- PIN + passphrase protection

### Trezor Security Model
- General-purpose microcontroller
- Fully open source (auditable by anyone)
- No Secure Element (philosophical choice)
- PIN + passphrase protection

**The debate:** Ledger's Secure Element provides physical tamper resistance. Trezor argues open source is more important because you can verify there's no backdoor.

**Reality:** Both are secure enough for 99.9% of users. The bigger risk is you losing your seed phrase or getting phished.

## Critical Setup Tips

**Write down your recovery phrase on paper.** Not a screenshot. Not a note on your phone. Paper. Store it somewhere safe, ideally in a fireproof location.

**Test your recovery phrase.** Before moving significant funds, wipe your device and restore from the phrase. If it works, you're good. If not, you caught the problem early.

**Buy from official sources only.**
- Ledger: shop.ledger.com
- Trezor: trezor.io

Never buy used hardware wallets. Scammers extract seed phrases, repackage, and sell. When you add funds, they drain them.

## The 2024 Ledger Recover Controversy

Ledger launched a controversial service called "Ledger Recover" that encrypts and splits your seed phrase among third parties. It sparked massive backlash.

**Our take:** Don't use it. Write down your seed phrase and store it safely. The whole point of a hardware wallet is that *only you* control the keys.
