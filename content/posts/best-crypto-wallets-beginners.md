## Understanding Wallet Types

Before choosing, understand the trade-offs:

### Hot Wallets (Software)
- Connected to the internet
- Free
- Convenient
- Vulnerable to hacks

### Cold Wallets (Hardware)
- Offline storage
- $50‚Äì$200 cost
- Less convenient
- Virtually unhackable

### Custodial vs Non-Custodial

**Custodial:** Someone else holds your keys (like an exchange). Easier, but you don't truly own the crypto.

**Non-custodial:** You hold your keys. True ownership, but total responsibility.

This guide focuses on non-custodial wallets‚Äîyou should control your own crypto.

## Critical Security Rules

### 1. Your Recovery Phrase is Everything

Anyone with your recovery phrase can steal your crypto. Write it on paper. Store it somewhere safe. Never:
- Screenshot it
- Email it
- Store it in cloud storage
- Share it with anyone

### 2. Verify Addresses

Before sending large amounts, send a small test amount first. Verify the address character by character.

### 3. Keep Software Updated

Wallet updates often include security patches. Enable auto-updates or check monthly.

### 4. Use 2FA Where Available

For hot wallets with 2FA, use Google Authenticator (not SMS).

### 5. Separate Wallets by Purpose

- **Hot wallet:** Small amounts for spending
- **Hardware wallet:** Savings, large amounts
- **Exchange:** Only what you're actively trading

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Storing Crypto on Exchanges Long-Term

Exchanges get hacked. They freeze accounts. Not your keys, not your coins.

### ‚ùå Buying Hardware Wallets from Amazon/eBay

Scammers extract seed phrases and resell. Buy only from official stores.

### ‚ùå Not Testing Recovery

Before moving significant funds, wipe your device and restore from your phrase. Make sure it works.

### ‚ùå Sharing Your Holdings

Don't make yourself a target. Keep your crypto holdings private.
