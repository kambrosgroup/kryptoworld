## Common Issues and Solutions

### Missing Transactions

**Problem:** Some transactions didn't import.

**Solution:** Most software lets you add manual transactions. Keep a spreadsheet backup of all your exchange exports.

### Wrong Cost Basis

**Problem:** Software shows gains/losses that don't match your records.

**Solution:** Check your cost basis method (FIFO is default). Make sure all transfers between your own wallets are marked correctly (not taxable events).

### DeFi Transactions Not Importing

**Problem:** Yield farming, staking, or liquidity pool transactions missing.

**Solution:** Enter your wallet addresses, not just exchange APIs. Some protocols require manual CSV uploads.

## Tax Tips for Crypto Investors

### 1. Track Everything

Every trade, every airdrop, every staking reward is a taxable event. The software only works if you give it complete data.

### 2. Understand Cost Basis

**FIFO (First In, First Out):** Oldest coins sold first. Usually results in higher gains (more tax).

**LIFO (Last In, First Out):** Newest coins sold first. Can reduce taxes in rising markets.

**HIFO (Highest In, First Out):** Highest cost basis sold first. Usually minimizes taxes.

Most software lets you choose. Consult a tax professional for your situation.

### 3. Harvest Tax Losses

Sell losing positions to offset gains. You can buy back after 30 days (wash sale rule doesn't apply to crypto... yet).

Koinly and TokenTax identify loss harvesting opportunities automatically.

### 4. Keep Records

Save your tax reports forever. The IRS can audit up to 6 years back. Crypto tax software keeps records, but have backups.
