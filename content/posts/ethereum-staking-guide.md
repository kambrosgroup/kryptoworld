## What Is Ethereum Staking?

Ethereum switched to Proof of Stake in 2022 ("The Merge"). Instead of miners using electricity to secure the network, validators stake ETH.

**How it works:**
1. You deposit 32 ETH to become a validator (or join a pool with less)
2. Your ETH helps process transactions and secure the network
3. You earn rewards (currently ~3â€“4% APY)
4. Rewards are paid in ETH every few days

**The risks:**
- Your ETH is locked (can't withdraw immediately)
- If your validator goes offline, you lose rewards
- If you act maliciously, you get "slashed" (lose ETH)

## Option 1: Solo Staking (For Technical Users)

**Requirements:**
- 32 ETH (~$80,000 at current prices)
- Dedicated computer (24/7 uptime)
- Stable internet connection
- Technical knowledge

**Pros:**
- Highest rewards (~4% APY)
- Maximum decentralization
- Full control
- No counterparty risk

**Cons:**
- High minimum (32 ETH)
- Technical complexity
- Penalties for downtime
- Hardware costs (~$1,000)

**Who should solo stake:**
- You have 32+ ETH
- You're technically competent
- You want maximum returns
- You believe in decentralization

**How to solo stake:**
1. Buy hardware (Intel NUC or similar, 2TB SSD, 32GB RAM)
2. Install Ethereum client (Prysm, Lighthouse, or Teku)
3. Generate validator keys
4. Deposit 32 ETH to official deposit contract
5. Monitor your validator 24/7

**Reality check:** Most people shouldn't solo stake. The technical requirements and penalties for mistakes make it risky for beginners.

## Liquid Staking: The Best of Both Worlds?

Liquid staking protocols give you a token representing your staked ETH. This token:
- Accrues staking rewards
- Can be traded on exchanges
- Can be used in DeFi (collateral, liquidity pools)

**Popular liquid staking tokens:**
- stETH (Lido)
- rETH (Rocket Pool)
- cbETH (Coinbase)
- BETH (Binance)

**The trade-off:** You accept smart contract risk in exchange for liquidity.

## Tax Implications

Staking rewards are taxable income in most jurisdictions.

**US:** Rewards are taxed as ordinary income when received. When you sell the rewarded ETH, you pay capital gains on any appreciation.

**Example:**
- You earn 0.1 ETH staking (worth $250 at receipt)
- You owe income tax on $250
- Later you sell that 0.1 ETH for $300
- You owe capital gains tax on $50

## Risks of Ethereum Staking

### 1. Lockup Risk

Your staked ETH isn't immediately withdrawable. While Ethereum now supports withdrawals, there may be queues during high demand.

### 2. Slashing Risk

If you're solo staking and your validator misbehaves (goes offline or acts maliciously), you lose ETH. Pools absorb this risk for you.

### 3. Smart Contract Risk

Liquid staking protocols use smart contracts. If there's a bug, funds could be lost. Stick to established protocols (Lido, Rocket Pool) with audited contracts.

### 4. Protocol Risk

Ethereum itself could have issues. While unlikely, major bugs or consensus failures could affect staked funds.

### 5. Tax Risk

Tax authorities are still figuring out staking. Rules could change, potentially retroactively.
